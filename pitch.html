02 · The Genre

Why build a friendslop game?

Games as a driver for social connection. Social gaming is shifting from winning to what happens between players.

Chaotic but accessible

Easy to pick up. Hard to keep it together. The skill ceiling is your group's ability to coordinate while laughing.

Moments that spark stories

Designed for shareable failure. Every flight produces a "you had to be there" moment worth retelling.

Built for streams & clips

Naturally generates clips, vertical chaos for TikTok, screams for YouTube reactions. The game does the marketing.

03 · Game Concept

Coordinated chaos in co-op.

A co-op game for 2–4 players where you must work together to complete objectives… while everything around you falls apart.

PlatformPC · Steam
ModeOnline
Players2 – 4
ToneFriendslop

It's not about doing things perfectly — it's about surviving the chaos together.

Engagement loop

The 4 verbs that drive every flight

01SERVE

Coffee, snacks, smiles. Keep passenger satisfaction green.

02REPAIR

Wings, fuel lines, cabin pressure. Tools, hands, panic.

03DEFEND

Birds, asteroids, angry passengers, smuggled livestock.

04FLY

Someone has to land this thing. Anyone. Please.

04 · Status

Built from a playable prototype.

We already validated the core. This isn't a slide deck pretending to be a game — it's a game pretending to be organized.

Real-time co-op dynamics

Roles, hand-offs, blame — all working today.

Physics-based interactions

Controlled chaos with rigid bodies & ragdolls. Predictably unpredictable.

Early proof of shared fun

Internal playtests already produced clip-worthy moments. Repeatedly.

Foundation for scale

Iterate fast, test with users, make informed decisions on scope.
